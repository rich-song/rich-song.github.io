Come-Out Roll:
- Roll 7 or 11: Pass Line wins
- Roll 2, 3, or 12: Pass Line loses (craps)
- Any other number (4,5,6,8,9,10) becomes the Point

Point Phase:
- Roll the Point again before 7: Pass Line wins
- Roll 7 before the Point: Pass Line loses (seven-out)
- All other rolls: no decision on Pass Line

Don't Pass: Opposite of Pass Line. 12 on come-out is a push (bar 12).

Come/Don't Come: Same as Pass/Don't Pass but placed after the point is set.

Place Bets: Bet that a specific number (4,5,6,8,9,10) will be rolled before 7. Active only when a point is established.

Field: Single-roll bet. Wins on 2,3,4,9,10,11,12. Pays double on 2 and 12.

Any 7: Single-roll bet that next roll is 7. Pays 4:1.

Any Craps: Single-roll bet that next roll is 2, 3, or 12. Pays 7:1.

Best Bets (Low House Edge):
1. Pass/Don't Pass + Odds - The odds bet has 0% house edge. Combined with pass line (1.41%), this is the best strategy in craps.

2. Place 6 or 8 - Only 1.52% house edge. Good supplemental bet.

3. Come with Odds - Spreads your action across multiple numbers with minimal house edge.

Worst Bets (High House Edge):
1. Any 7 - 16.67% house edge
2. Any Craps - 11.11% house edge
3. Hardways - 9-11% house edge
4. Field - 5.56% house edge

Strategy Tips:
- Always take maximum odds on Pass/Come bets
- The more you bet on odds, the lower your overall house edge
- Place 6 and 8 are the best place bets
- Avoid single-roll proposition bets
- Set a win/loss limit and stick to it
- $5 Pass Line + max odds is the classic low-edge strategy
